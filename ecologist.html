Ecologist

Description

Ecology is a branch of biology that is the science of how organisms in environments interact with one another as well as the habitats they live in. An ecologist studies these relationships between organisms and environments and makes important scientific contributions and discoveries. An ecologist may spend time in a laboratory or office, or out in the field collecting data.

Associated Majors and Type of Degrees

To become an ecologist, a bachelors degree is needed at minuimum with higher level jobs requiring a masters degree. Degrees in zoology, wildlife biology and environmental science are useful for getting a job as an ecologist.

Helping Qualifications - Bachelor’s degree in environmental science, biology, chemistry, physics, geosciences, or engineering, master’s degree helps when trying to advance in your career path

Popular Courses - Biology, chemistry, geology, physics, hydrology, waste management, and environmental policy/regulations

Security Clearance?

A research ecologist working for the department of agriculture would be required to maintain a security clearance, because they have the potential to be working with sensitive information that couldbe harmful if it was placed in the wrong hands. Also they may need a security clearance if they are working on or accessing secret land in a country.

Interesting Facts

In 2017, the median pay for ecologists/zoologists was $62,290 per year!
